    About this dashboard

    This is an interactive view of monthly and annual income statistics published by Georgia's Revenue Service (rs.ge). Use the filters to slice by year, month and income type, then explore distribution, inequality and trends.

    Glossary

    • Median — the income that splits the population in half. Half earn less, half earn more.
    • Mean — total taxable income divided by people. Pulled up by very high earners.
    • Gini coefficient — 0 means everyone earns the same, 1 means one person has everything. Most countries land between 0.25 and 0.55.
    • Lorenz curve — plots cumulative income share vs cumulative population share. The diagonal is perfect equality.
    • Percentile — pX means X% of people earn less than this amount.

    Caveats

    • The data is bracketed, so all percentile calculations linearly interpolate within a bracket.
    • The open-ended top bracket (>₾100,000) is anchored by its average — actual top-percentile values may be higher.
    • Income marked "All" sums every reported income type. "Salary" is wages only.
